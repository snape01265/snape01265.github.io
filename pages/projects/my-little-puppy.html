Experience and Feelings

Working on My Little Puppy at Dreamotion was my first professional project and a massive leap from my student days. Collaborating with a team of up to 30 people taught me how to work within established pipelines, communicate with other team members with different roles (not just engineers), and deliver production quality work under tight deadlines. And by tight deadlines, I mean one to two days per content/function level of TIGHT deadlines.

Participating in the development and experiencing the full cycle of a game project until its release was quite an experience. Unlike the indie level of games I have worked on so far, creating a big commercial game made me realize the complexity and scale of such projects. Also, trying a 3D platformer gave me new insights as well as tough challenges in programming that I enjoyed solving.

Shipping on multiple platforms gave me some insight into platform specific development that I wouldn't have experienced anywhere else. Towards the tail end of development, I had the opportunity to try out different fields of work (like DevOps, PM, Localizing, and Game Design) while porting the game to different platforms that I would have otherwise not experienced.
